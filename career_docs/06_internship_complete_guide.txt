DOCUMENT: Complete Internship Guide for Engineering Students in India

=== WHY INTERNSHIPS MATTER ===
- Internships add real experience to your resume before your first job.
- 60-70% of PPO (Pre-Placement Offers) come from internships at top companies.
- Government internship certificates help in PSU applications.
- You learn skills not taught in college - real tools, real projects, real deadlines.

=== TOP INTERNSHIP PLATFORMS IN INDIA ===

1. INTERNSHALA (internshala.com)
Best for: All engineering branches, especially for students in Tier 2/3 colleges.
Internship types: Work from home, on-site, part-time, stipend-based.
Stipend range: 5,000 - 30,000 per month.
How to use: Create a detailed profile, add skills, apply to 20-30 internships simultaneously.
Tip: Filter by "recently posted" and apply within 24 hours for best chances.

2. LINKEDIN (linkedin.com)
Best for: Tech companies, product companies, MNCs.
Stipend range: 15,000 - 1,00,000 per month.
How to use: Optimize your LinkedIn profile with skills, projects, certifications. Use "Easy Apply". Connect with HR and recruiters.
Tip: Message recruiters directly with a short personalized note. 

3. WELLFOUND / ANGELLIST (wellfound.com)
Best for: Startups and product companies.
Stipend range: 10,000 - 50,000 per month.
How to use: Create profile, browse startups, apply directly.

4. UNSTOP (unstop.com, formerly Dare2Compete)
Best for: Hackathons, case competitions, sponsored internships.
Tip: Winning or placing in hackathons gives you a direct fast-track to internship offers.

5. NAUKRI.COM
Best for: Core engineering jobs (Mechanical, Civil, Electrical).
Stipend range: 5,000 - 20,000 per month.

6. LETSINTERN (letsintern.com)
Best for: Early-stage students (1st and 2nd year).

7. FORAGE (theforage.com)
Best for: Free virtual internship programs from top companies like JP Morgan, Goldman Sachs, Accenture.
These are self-paced, free, and give a certificate. Great for freshers.

8. HACKEREARTH / HACKERRANK
Best for: Tech internships with coding tests.
Tip: Maintain a good HackerRank profile and solve problems regularly.

=== GOVERNMENT INTERNSHIP PROGRAMS ===

1. DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organisation)
Eligibility: 3rd year onwards.
Duration: 4-8 weeks.
Stipend: 10,000-12,000/month.
Apply: Through drdo.gov.in portal or college placement cell.
Branches: Electronics, Mechanical, Computer Science, Electrical.

2. ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation)
Eligibility: 3rd/4th year B.Tech or M.Tech students.
Duration: 4-8 weeks.
Stipend: 10,000-15,000/month.
Apply: ISRO website, internship section.
Branches: Electronics, Mechanical, Computer Science.

3. IOCL (Indian Oil Corporation Limited)
Eligibility: 2nd/3rd year students in Chemical, Mechanical, Civil, Instrumentation.
Duration: 4-6 weeks summer internship.
Stipend: 10,000-15,000/month.
Apply: IOCL official careers portal.

4. NTPC (National Thermal Power Corporation)
Eligibility: Engineering students any year.
Duration: 4-8 weeks.
Branches: Electrical, Mechanical, Civil, Electronics.
Stipend: 10,000/month.

5. BHEL (Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited)
Eligibility: 2nd-3rd year students.
Branches: Mechanical, Electrical, Electronics, Civil, IT.
Duration: 4-8 weeks.

6. CDAC (Centre for Development of Advanced Computing)
Best for: Computer Science and Electronics students.
Projects in: AI, cybersecurity, cloud computing, embedded systems.

7. NIC (National Informatics Centre)
Best for: IT and Computer Science students.
Projects in: Government software projects, e-governance.

=== HOW TO PREPARE YOUR INTERNSHIP APPLICATION ===

RESUME TIPS:
- Keep to 1 page only.
- Include: Name, contact, education, skills, projects, certifications, achievements.
- Quantify everything: "Built ML model with 92% accuracy" not "Built an ML model".
- Add GitHub profile link and LinkedIn profile link.
- Use simple, clean format - no fancy colors or tables.
- Tailor resume for each application - highlight relevant skills.

COVER LETTER TIPS (if required):
- First paragraph: Why you want THIS specific internship.
- Second paragraph: What skills and experience you bring.
- Third paragraph: What you hope to learn. Keep it to 150-200 words.

INTERVIEW PREPARATION:
For Tech Internships:
- Practice DSA on LeetCode (easy to medium problems).
- Know your resume projects deeply - be ready to explain every line of code.
- Prepare 2-3 project demos.

For Core Engineering Internships:
- Revise fundamentals of your branch.
- Know about the company's products and recent news.
- Prepare answers: "Tell me about yourself", "Why this company?", "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"

=== BRANCH-WISE BEST INTERNSHIP TYPES ===

CSE/IT: Software development, data science, ML, web development, cybersecurity.
ECE: Embedded systems, IoT, VLSI design, RF engineering, signal processing.
Mechanical: Automotive design, manufacturing, product design, CAD.
Civil: Construction management, structural design, urban planning, surveying.
Electrical: Power systems, automation, control systems, renewable energy.
Chemical: Process engineering, petroleum, pharmaceuticals, food processing.

=== TIPS TO MAXIMISE INTERNSHIP SUCCESS ===
1. Treat it like a full-time job - be punctual, professional.
2. Ask questions - you are there to learn.
3. Maintain a daily log of what you do - useful for resume and interviews later.
4. Network with seniors and engineers at the company.
5. Request a letter of recommendation before leaving.
6. Ask for PPO (Pre-Placement Offer) if your performance is good.
7. Document your project well to showcase in future interviews.
